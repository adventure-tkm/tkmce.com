College Union

Manaveeyam Union Inaguration Flim Fest First Love Athletic Meet Women's Day Kite Fest Holi Celebration Fests

The TKMCE College Union has been the backbone and driving force of the students of the institution ever since its inception. The student body strives to enhance the academic, technical, cultural and athletic reach of the students. The Union always panders to the needs of the students and articulates them unto the faculty. Over the years, it has earned the trust of faculty, management and most of all, the students, with their consistency and impeccable leadership. The council yearns for innovation and lights a flame of brotherhood in all.

Manaveeyam

Gallery ☰

Manaveeyam was held on November 1st, 2018, the day of Kerala Piravi. Competitions like Tug of War, traditionally called ‘Vadamvali’ and ‘Malayali Manka’ were held on the day adding zeal to the celebrations. Donations were made to organizations of several needs.The day celebrated togetherness and harmony amongst all Keralites.

Union Inaguration

Gallery ☰

The college union inauguration for the academic year 2018-19, “Aagathi”, was held on 13th November 2018. The programme was officially inaugurated by Honorable Chief Guest Ms Illakiya IAS, sub- collector of Kollam. The newly elected student union took the oath. Logo for literary and debate club was launched by the chief guest. We also honored our teachers who volunteered to lead the students in the flood relief activities. Followed by the Union inauguration was the Arts club inauguration done by playback singer Mr. K S Harishankar. About 50 cultural programs were staged after the inaugural function.

Film Festival

Gallery ☰

The Film and Drama club of TKMCE conducted the Film Festival from 24th January to the 27th. Officially inaugurated in the name TKMFF’19 the event was graced by critically celebrated director and writer V C ABHILASH and imminent actor, director and producer PRAKASH BARE along with ADITI BALAN, Aruvi fame and SHAMMI THILAKAN. An informative and interactive session with the dignitaries served answers to many doubts. This fest was conducted in association with Kerala State Chalachithra Academy (KSCA) and Women in Cinema Collective (WCC). The fest was also filled in with numerous workshops and talks along with the movies. A diverse collection of movies were screened during the festival. Various corners of our college such as the Auditorium, Jubilee Hall etc. held the screening. The Fall and Tumbadd were movies that had left the audience in awe.

National Level Kite flying festival

Gallery ☰

The Kite Festival at Kollam Beach was organized by the students of TKMCE as a pre event to Hestia’19, their upcoming techno-cultural fest. Over 2000 students from TKMCE and many more outsiders who arrived at the beach took part in an attempt at the Asian record for most kites flown simultaneously. The crowd was successful in their record-breaking endeavor and the certificate was duly presented to the crowd by the officials of the URF followed by a huge round of cheer and applause.

This event was organized with the aim of highlighting the severe environmental issues at Munroe Islands. Situated in the inner channels of Kerala’s backwaters, Munroe Islands are a string of eight islets at the convergence of Ashtamudi Lake and Kallada river. Rapid climate change and rising water levels have led to hundreds of homes being abandoned and even more families being displaced. The festival was organized as an awareness campaign about the plight of Munroe and its residents and the need for adopting a more scientific approach in construction.

This festival had also successfully managed to instill a sense of camaraderie among all the participants. The thousands of kites that veiled the blue skies like a kaleidoscope served as a reminder that when people stand by each other in working towards a common goal, the sky truly is the limit!

Hestia 19

Gallery ☰

Hestia’19, the Techno- Cultural symposium of TKM College of Engineering was inaugurated by Padma Bhushan Dr. Nambi Narayanan and Padma Shri Balachandra Menon on the 28th of March, 2019 at the college auditorium. The dignitaries jointly lighted the lamp and inaugurated the four-day festival. Padma Shri Balachandra Menon, in his address, talked about the hardships one faces in life and how they help a person grow while Padma Bhushan Dr. Nambi Narayanan talked about the importance of dreams and hopes a student has. The event was also graced by Dr. T. K. Shahal Hassan, Chairman, TKM Trust and The Principal, Dr. S. Ayoob. The winners of the ‘All Terrain Vehicle – XLR8(SAE)’ competition were also felicitated. The Asian Record won for the most number of Kites flown simultaneously at the National Kite Festival organised as part of Hestia’19 on March 17th was presented to Dr. T. K. Shahal Hassan Musaliar by Dr. S. Ayoob. Following the award ceremony, Principal Dr. S. Ayoob, College Union Chairman Shri Mohamed Anser and General Secretary Shri Aswin P.S. addressed the gathering.
